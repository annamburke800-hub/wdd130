Client report

When I was making my site plan, I thought about what my topic was, and chose what content and images to include. My site was about barbershop quartets, so I included information about the style, history, and modern competitions of barbershop music, and I included relevant pictures.

Me and my developer interacted quite a bit. When he had questions about the site plan, he emailed me and I was able to respond with the answers.

As of writing this, my developers have definitely met my expectations for my site. Everything is laid out the way I specified, and it looks really nice as well!

Next time I hire a team to do a project for me, I will definitely start communicating earlier. In this project, we didn't start emailing until a week or two into the project, and I think that really hindered us.

Lead Developer Report

I feel I managed my development team well. At the start of the project, I delegated one page for each of us, and I kept in regular contact with both of my junior developers throughout.

I communicated with my client beautifully! I emailed him with my questions, and he emailed me back with clarifications. Because we mostly communicated through email, however, there were a few moments where one of us would send an email and the other wouldn't see it until a few days later, which caused some frustration. But other than that, it went great.

I feel I handled it well. I put the site on GitHub as soon as I could, and I regularly updated it with new work, and I sent the link to the website to my client. Overall, it went well.

Coming into this class, I was worried about hosting my own website, but it was a lot easier than I thought! GitHub makes it so easy to host a website, and I had a great time with this project.

Junior Developer Report

I had great communication with both of my team leads. Almost every class period we would talk at least a little bit about the website and what we needed to do.

There were points where the wireframes I was given didn't make sense, but at those times I would just talk to my lead developer, and I would get clarifications. Once I had all the information I needed, I knew for sure what was expected of me for each site.

I was! I was able to make the menu page on 'Bagel Blitz' and the everyday miracles page on 'Finding Every Day's Miracle.' I'm proud of how these pages came out, and I hope the clients like them as well.

Everything was pretty intuitive, so I didn't run into any issues when adding my changes to GitHub.

General Reflection

The most challenging part of this project was the uncertainty. I don't have the clients' visions in my head, so I was frustrated when the site plan didn't have enough information on it. Luckily, by communicating with the people on my team, I was able to ask the questions I had and get everything sorted out.

My favorite part of this project was making the pages themselves! Once I had all the information I needed, I was able to just go and make the website, which was a lot of fun!

Again, I would put more emphasis on communication between everyone. Every problem I had was solved quiclkly with clear communication, so I would start that conversation earlier with my team.

I feel a lot more confident! Now I know that I can work with others on one project, and this makes me a lot more excited for the websites I'll make in the future. I've learned a lot from this project, and I'll take these lessons with me into the future.
